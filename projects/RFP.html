RED FOX PRESS

RedFoxPress was created to enable a group of artists to sell their work online. The site includes prints, photographs, and books located in Achill Island in the West of Ireland.

Problem:

The client wants to increase site traffic and the number of purchases made online. Due to the current digital presence and web functions, not many users would want to browse online, user can’t search keywords for product, hard to read and even to understand what this website was trying to selling.

Solution:

Improve the digital presence and expand its reach by redesigning the website. Being able to help users to find the type of artwork they want, increase traffic and a greater number of purchases made online.

Proto Persona

We targeted users and audiences on art student with low budget based on the assumptions of stakeholders. It allows us to begin designing and building immediately without getting overly bogged down with the details of user behavior. We created a profile to understand their needs and challenges better the persona helped us which better solutions due to a deeper understanding of users' pain points, needs, goals, and general characteristics.

5 Seconds Impression Test

We started off by conducting a 5 seconds impression test based on the orginal website to gather more rellevent opionion. It helps our team to measure what information users take away and what impression they get within the first five seconds of viewing a design.

Affinity Diagram

We grabbed our interview feedback and grouped them into individual sections. We organized many ideas into their natural relationships from a brainstorming session. Use it to generate, manage, and consolidate information related to a product, process, complex issue, or problem.

Empathy Map

We use this method to gain a deeper insight into our customers. What they might say, think, does, and feels to learn about their behaviors and decisions. It externalizes knowledge about users in order to create a shared understanding of users' needs and aid in decision-making.
